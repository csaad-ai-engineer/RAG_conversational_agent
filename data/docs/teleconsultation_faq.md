# Teleconsultation FAQ

## What do patients need to join a teleconsultation?
A stable internet connection, a device with a camera and microphone (laptop, tablet, or smartphone),
and an up-to-date browser. No app installation is required; the video call runs directly in-browser.

## When does the video call link become active?
The teleconsultation link becomes active 10 minutes before the scheduled appointment time and stays
active for the duration of the booked slot.

## What happens if the connection drops during a teleconsultation?
Both the patient and the practitioner can rejoin using the same link. If the disconnection lasts more
than 5 minutes, the practitioner may choose to convert the remaining time into a phone call.

## Are teleconsultations reimbursed the same way as in-person visits?
Reimbursement depends on the patient's health insurance provider and the practitioner's registered
teleconsultation status. Practitioners must have teleconsultation enabled in their profile settings
for the visit to be eligible for standard reimbursement.
